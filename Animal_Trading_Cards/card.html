Iguana

A couple species of lizard within the genus Iguana possess a tiny "third eye" on their heads.

  • Scientific Name: Iguana iguana
  • Average Length: 1.5 - 1.8 meters (5 - 6 ft)
  • Average Lifespan: 20 years
  • Habitat: Rain forests of northern Mexico, Central America, Caribbean Islands and southern Brazil

Iguanas can grow from 1.5 to 1.8 meters (5 to 6 ft.) in length from head to tail. The have scales that cover the entire body, with spikes that run down the spine through the tail. The scales coloration allows the Iguana to blend in with its surroundings with a colorful palette of tans, blues and greens. They also possess a dewlap that is on the underside of the head running down the throat. Although they appear clumsy they are can move across the terrain at 21 miles per hour.
